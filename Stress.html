STRESS MANAGEMENT

What is Stress?


Stress, one of the most commonly used words by everyone. Whether it is a teenager, a working professional, a housewife, or a student uses this word once a day.


But what is stress? Why has it become so common? Why are online counselling centers increasing to help people with stress issues?


There are so many questions on which we don’t focus. We keep living our life normally and the level of stress keeps increasing every day because there is no cure done. Some people even feel that there is no cure needed for stress
as it is a part of life.


Yes, it is normal to be stressed. But what if you are not able to think, sleep, perform well in your day to day life properly due to stress?.

This might be a high time for you to take a proper medical guidance

What Are The Symptoms Of Stress?


Symptoms of stress vary from one person to another. Stress can have mental as well as physical symptoms, some people see these symptoms but do not take precautions to cure stress and end up having other mental health disorders.


Some of the physical symptoms of stress are – Hypertensions, headache, body pain, chronic pain, insomnia, digestive issues, and diabetes.

Some of the mental symptoms of stress are – Depression, anxiety, addictions, food, and eating issues.


Not everyone feels both mental and physical symptoms. The level and intensity to feel these symptoms can be different for everyone. Sometimes stress is positive too. Such as when it is the first day for someone to join the office the stress can be on the highest level but the excitement and happiness of having a secured job can reduce the level of stress.


How To Manage Stress?


Following are some of the resources that will make you feel good 😊 : –

  • Exercise:
    Having a simple workout can drastically change how you feel. It can deviate your mind from stress casuing things in your life. In the mean time you can enjoy the life to the fullest without being worried.

  • Deep breathing:
    It increases Oxygen level in blood. Increased blood Oxygen level can help work brain with its fullest capacity. It can also help in preventing mood swings. We all know how much important Blood Oxygen Level is in this pandemic period! Follow this practice atleast 10 minutes a day and feel the change!

  • Talking to someone about your problems:
    Studies have proven that confronting your problems can help in reucing the stress. Also the person might come up with a solution to you stress and you might have a peaceful life after that 🤞

  • Eating healthy:
    Studies have shown that people eating junk food succumb to dangerous diseases like Diabetes, High BP etc. and these diseases might lead to mental stress. Eating "Ma ke hath ka khana" is the best way to stay fit and healthy!

  • Avoiding alcohol and smoking:
    • These drug can infiltrate with the nervous system and produce depression. Its best to stay away from any kind of drugs.

FAQ's


How Do I Get Rid Of Stress?

→ You can practice exercising, writing down your problems, keeping yourself busy, meditating, and if stress is not going from life trying to get in touch with a professional for stress management counselling.


What Therapy Is Best For Stress?

→ So far, Cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT) has been the best therapy for stress. Which helps you in identifying your thought patterns and triggers and taking positive actions.


How Stress Affects Physical And Mental Health?

→ Stress can give a person a lot of mental and physical health issues such as anger issues, depression, chronic pain, insomnia, etc.


What Is The Best Exercise For Stress?

→ Some of the best exercises to reduce stress are yoga, dancing, outdoor activities, kickboxing, etc. These exercises help in maintaining the physical and mental discipline to help you relax.
